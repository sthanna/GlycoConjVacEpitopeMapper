Early \nstructure-informed approaches improved interpretation by mapping single-site \nmetrics onto protein structures, and later methods introduced 3D sliding windows \nto capture spatial neighbourhoods missed by linear analyses. These frameworks, \nhowever, are restricted to predefined statistics and narrowly defined 3D window \ntypes, limiting the scope of questions that can be addressed. We developed an R \npackage, evo3D, a generalised framework for structure-informed evolutionary \nanalysis that supports any downstream statistic and scales from simple to \ncomplex structures. evo3D extracts structure-informed multiple sequence \nalignment subsets (spatial haplotypes), making the structure-informed unit of \nanalysis directly available to users. The framework provides fixed-count and \nfixed-distance spatial windows, supports residue and codon analysis modes, and \nextends across multimers, interfaces, and multiple structural models through a \nsingle wrapper, run_evo3d(). We demonstrate evo3D\'s utility by performing an \nepitope-level diversity scan of Hepatitis C virus E1/E2 complex, identifying \nconserved spatial neighbourhoods missed by linear sliding windows, and by \nevaluating evo3D\'s scalability on the octameric Chikungunya virus E1/E2 \nassembly. By formalising the core components of structure-informed genetic \nanalysis and removing the technical barriers, evo3D streamlines the evaluation \nof evolutionary patterns directly within 3D structural contexts and we \nanticipate its wide application in molecular evolution studies.
