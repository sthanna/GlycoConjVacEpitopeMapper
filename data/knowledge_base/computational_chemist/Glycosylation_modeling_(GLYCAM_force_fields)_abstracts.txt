The \ncovalent linkage of a carbohydrate to the nitrogen atom of an asparagine, a \nprocess referred to as N-linked glycosylation, plays an important role in the \nphysiology of many living organisms. Most software for glycan modeling on a \npersonal desktop computer requires knowledge of molecular dynamics to interface \nwith specialized programs such as CHARMM or AMBER. There are a number of popular \nweb-based tools that are available for modeling glycans (e.g., GLYCAM-WEB \n(http:// https://dev.glycam.org/gp/ ) or Glycosciences.db ( \nhttp://www.glycosciences.de/ )). The GUI provides \na straightforward two-dimensional (2D) rendering of a glycoprotein that allows \nfor a quick visual inspection of the glycosylation state of all the sequons on a \nprotein structure. Modeled glycans can be further refined by a genetic algorithm \nfor removing clashes and sampling alternative conformations.
