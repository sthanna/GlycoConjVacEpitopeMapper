## Key Themes in Included Literature

### 1. Carbohydrate B-cell Epitope Identification
The dominant paradigm — polysaccharide antigens generate T-independent (TI-2) B-cell responses. Key methods:
- **X-ray crystallography & cryo-EM**: Structural resolution of antibody–antigen complexes (Fab fragments with oligosaccharide haptens)
- **STD-NMR / tr-NOESY**: Solution-state epitope mapping of carbohydrate–lectin/antibody interactions
- **Glycan microarrays**: High-throughput profiling of antibody binding specificity across defined oligosaccharide libraries

### 2. T-cell Help in Glycoconjugate Vaccines
Carrier proteins (CRM197, TT, DT, PD) provide T-helper epitopes essential for class switching:
- Carrier protein selection is a critical determinant of T-cell epitope presentation
- O-acetylation status of polysaccharide affects both B-cell epitope exposure and processing
- Recent work: in-cell depolymerization pathways determine what glycan fragments are presented on MHC-II

### 3. Computational Epitope Prediction
Emerging methods reviewed:
- **AlphaFold3 / ESM-2**: Predicting glycoprotein structure for epitope accessibility modeling
- **Molecular dynamics**: Conformational sampling of flexible polysaccharide antigens
- **Machine learning**: Predicting immunodominant carbohydrate motifs from glycan array data

### 4. Synthetic Carbohydrate Vaccines (Fully Defined Epitopes)
Fully synthetic approach allows precise epitope definition:
- Chemical synthesis of defined oligosaccharides (2–8 repeat units) conjugated to carriers
- SAR studies identifying minimal protective epitope length
- 2026 examples: Pseudomonas aeruginosa, S. aureus, Acinetobacter baumannii synthetic vaccines

### 5. Functional Correlates
- **OPA (opsonophagocytic activity)**: Gold standard functional readout for protective antibody
- **Bactericidal assays**: Complement-dependent killing as secondary endpoint
- **Avidity index**: Measures antibody maturation quality post-vaccination

## Research Gaps Identified

1. **Standardization of epitope definition methods** — No consensus on minimal protective saccharide length across pathogens
2. **Structural data for polysaccharide-antibody complexes** — Cryo-EM structures remain sparse compared to protein antigens  
3. **AI/ML for carbohydrate epitope prediction** — AlphaFold doesn't model glycans; dedicated tools nascent
4. **Cross-reactive epitopes** — Understudied; potential for broader-spectrum vaccines
5. **Neonatal/pediatric immune responses** — Epitope recognition may differ from adults; limited data
